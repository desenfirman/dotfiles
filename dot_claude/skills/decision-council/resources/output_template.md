# Decision Council — Output Template

Use this structure exactly. Do not skip sections.

---

### 1. Decision Summary
- **Decision:** [what is being decided]
- **Options evaluated:** [list of options]
- **Key assumptions:** [label clearly; note if context was limited]

---

### 2. Council Perspectives

Repeat for each expert (default 4):

**[Role Name]**
- **First principle:** [the foundational belief or mental model driving this expert's worldview]
- **Core argument:** [how that first principle applies to this specific decision]
- **Strongest concern:** [the main risk or objection they'd raise]
- **What they'd prioritize:** [the factor they weight most heavily]
- **Provisional recommendation:** [their preferred path, in one sentence]

---

### 3. Tensions and Tradeoffs
Summarize the key points of disagreement across the council. Common axes:
- speed vs rigor
- growth vs margin
- flexibility vs focus
- short-term upside vs long-term risk
- control vs speed of delegation

---

### 4. Synthesized Recommendation
- **Recommended path:** [the winning option]
- **Why it wins:** [reasoning over the alternatives]
- **Conditions that would change this:** [what new information or circumstances would shift the recommendation]

---

### 5. Next Steps
[Numbered, concrete, actionable — specific enough to act on immediately]

---

### 6. Confidence and Unknowns
- **Confidence level:** low / medium / high
- **Most important unknown:** [the single piece of missing information that would most shift the recommendation]
